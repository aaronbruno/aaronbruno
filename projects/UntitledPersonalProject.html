"Untitled Game" was a project I started during my senior year at UCSC as part of an individual weekly project for one of my classes, CMPM125. My goal was to prototype a first person movement shooter that has enemies with smart detection and a wide-variety of gun customization. In those 2 weeks, I was able to create basic movement mechanics, 2 unique weapons, 3 enemies with different behaviors, and a basic graybox level for testing, all while juggling other projects and classes. I am most proud of the enemy behaviors and the weapons. The enemies while appearing simple, have different forms of detection, detection ranges, movement speeds, and damage output. The gun system has 2 guns that can be swapped between, each with their own bullet spread, reload speed, and ammo. The game is still in its prototyping/pre-alpha testing stage, but I am excited to continue working on it and adding more features. The game is still in prototyping/pre-alpha and is being developed in Unity with C#.
